How a lead moves from Typeform to webinar, 1-on-1, reminders, and follow-up.

This is the operational map for the current Beforest sales funnel. Boxes show the system doing the work. Notes explain what your sales team needs to look for inside Pipedrive.

Typeform = lead source Windmill = automation brain Pipedrive = CRM truth Azure AI = fit check Google Calendar = final 1-on-1 booking Calendly = webinar + Vivek slots Zoom = webinar registration Interakt = WhatsApp Human = sales/admin action

What sales should know

Owner routing is automatic from the collective selected in the Typeform. A Mumbai or Bhopal lead should land with Vivekanand. A Hammiyala or Poomaale lead should land with Sai Rakesh.

Important subtlety

Route decision:

  1. Deal goes to Lead_In.
  2. Label becomes Scheduled Webinar Pending.
  3. Webinar reminder path takes over after Calendly booking.

  1. Deal stays in Initial Event Scheduled.
  2. Label becomes 1on1 TBC.

Pipedrive activity style

Intake logs are intentionally short: Typeform received, with notes like collective, fit result, and whether the deal was created or updated.

Sales action when stuck

If you see Review fit manually, read the Typeform answers. If fit, move to Initial Event Scheduled and apply 1on1 TBC. If not fit, keep the webinar route.

Mostly from callback data.
Pipedrive
Updates label/activity
Only real button clicks change workflow state.

Lead clicks Reschedule Call

  1. Windmill finds the existing calendar event.
  2. Calendar event is cancelled.
  3. Meeting fields are cleared.
  4. Deal moves to 1on1 Awaiting.
  5. Lead receives owner booking link.

Lead clicks Confirm Attendance

  1. Deal moves into 1on1 Pending.
  2. Lead gets a short confirmation WhatsApp.
  3. Reminder and owner-check loops can pick it up.

WhatsApp delivery failed

  1. Status events never trigger reschedule.
  2. Failure creates open task WhatsApp failed to deliver.
  3. Error code and reason are shown for sales/admin.

Why this matters

Interakt sends both user actions and delivery status updates. Status events may contain the words Reschedule Call inside the template body, but Windmill now ignores those as actions unless they are actual button replies.

Sales view

Look for simple activity subjects: Booking link sent on WhatsApp, Attendance confirmed on WhatsApp, WhatsApp failed to deliver.

Purpose

This is a safety net for Zoom collective introductions. It keeps Zoom registrations visible in Pipedrive even when the main Typeform/Calendly path is not the source.

Sales caveat

Duplicate deals can be acceptable when the same person signs up for different collectives. The key check is that the deal is in the right collective pipeline and owner.

Short activity subjects are the operational language.

Review fit manuallyAI fit check failed; human must decide fit/unfit.
1-on-1 call with {{name}}Real owner-owned open meeting activity.
Calendar invite createdAutomation created the owner calendar event.
Prebooked call message sentWhatsApp was accepted by Interakt.
WhatsApp failed to deliverInterakt reported delivery failure, with code and reason.
Booking link sent on WhatsAppLead received link after reschedule/no-slot fallback.
Owner marked attendedOwner confirmed attendance from WhatsApp.

Golden rule

Pipedrive should answer: what happened, what the lead received, what the owner needs to do, and what the next automation is waiting on.

When unsure

Look at the latest open activity first. Open activities usually mean a human needs to act. Done activities usually explain what the system already did.
